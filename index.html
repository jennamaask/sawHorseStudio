// The Low Down

Saw Horse Studio was founded by Jenna Maaskant in 2016. Jenna is a kitesurfing-addicted, salsa-dancing, yoga-practicing, cactus-loving world wonderer who, despite her best efforts, always seems to come back home to toil away in her wood shop. Every one-of-a-kind piece produced at Saw Horse is created from trees salvaged on her family farm in rural southwestern Ontario. We are proud to say that we wait for trees to live out their natural life span before turning them into beautiful pieces of furniture.

Lucky for us, good things come to those who wait.

In this case that good thing is spalted wood. Spalted wood has beautiful pronounced black or white "ink" markings in patterns only mother nature could paint. Spalting is an indication of the initial stage of decay of a tree. What makes useable spalted wood so rare is that is must be harvested during a very short time window before the wood is left soft or rotted.

Fortunately, we seem to have a thing for timing.

// Process

nº 1

seek + salvage

All the lumber we use at Saw Horse Studio is harvested from our very own property. We let nature do it's thing, and wait patiently for trees or branches to fall down on their own accord. With regular walks through the woods we try to spot a perished tree as soon as it's fallen.

nº 2

mill + dry

Once the tree is removed from the forest we strapped them down onto our wagon and bring them over to a local sawmill to be cut into slabs. Once the slabs are returned to the farm we begin the long and slow process of air-drying the wood. We stack the wood in a dry corner of our shop and leave it for 2-3 years. As the wood drys out it warps, twists, and contracts. It's important to let this process be fully completed before we start working on a piece.

nº 3

plane + sand

When the wood is dry the fun can begin! We first plane the wood with a router to get rid of any warps, dips or bumps. After that we start sanding. All live edges are sanded only by hand to ensure the natural features remain intact. We use a combination of belt sanders and palm sanders to smooth of the top and bottom of the slab until it feels like glass.

nº 4

design + protect

After the piece has taken shape we do a little design work. If we are making a table we design the stainless steel legs and have them custom built by a local manufacturing business. It's then time to put on a finish to protect and preserve. With our tables we usually use a water-based polyurethane, and for the charcuterie boards an Osmo® food safe top coat is applied. Both of these products allow for the piece to keep its natural colouring, without darkening or yellowing.
